The core problem being solved is the lack of English learning tools that support Sinhala as a base language. Apps like Duolingo exist for many languages, but none cater specifically to Sinhala speakers. This app aims to fill that gap with a fully AI-driven, voice only approach that adapts to each user’s proficiency level.  Core Concept How it works The app functions as a conversational AI tutor. A user who speaks no English at all can begin by speaking entirely in Sinhala. The AI responds in Sinhala initially, then gradually introduces English words and phrases over time as the user’s confidence and ability grows. This mirrors immersion-based learning but in a controlled, adaptive, and nonintimidating way. Progression model The progression is not mechanical (e.g. one new word per day). Instead, the AI continuously assesses the user’s actual spoken English ability during each conversation. It determines when the user is ready to handle more English and shifts the language ratio accordingly. The goal is organic, natural progression. Sinhala-heavy at first, shifting to mostly English over time. Users who think they can already speak English may be assessed and placed at a more advanced starting point. The AI handles this automatically.  Conversation Design Topic restrictions The app is not a general-purpose chatbot or therapy tool. Conversations are restricted to safe, light, everyday topics to avoid users sharing sensitive personal information. Approved topic areas include: How was your day? What do you think about the weather? General daily routines and experiences Topics involving personal location, financial details, health issues, or other sensitive areas are out of scope. Speaking pace and silence handling The AI is patient and non-interruptive. It gives the user adequate time to compose and speak their sentences. If a user pauses mid-sentence or goes silent for a defined period (the exact timer to be finalized during development), the AI gently prompts or offers feedback on what was said rather than cutting them off. This is critical for learners who need extra time to find their words. Pronunciation feedback After the user finishes speaking (or after the silence timer triggers), the AI can provide gentle correction on pronunciation and grammar. Corrections happen at the end of the user’s turn, not mid-sentence, to avoid disrupting the flow of conversation. Voice only The app is intentionally voice-only. There is no text input option. This keeps the user focused on the core goal: speaking English out loud. Mixing typing and speaking would dilute the learning objective. Progress Tracking & Gamification The app provides full, transparent visibility of the user’s learning journey. Progress tracking includes: Daily and weekly session logs A calendar view showing consistency (like streak systems in apps like Duolingo or fitness apps) A clear indication of the user’s current English level and how they have progressed over time Session performance indicators (e.g. how much English was used vs Sinhala in today’s conversation) The tracking system is designed to be motivating rather than punishing. It celebrates improvement and keeps users aware of their progress.  Monetization Strategy Freemium model The app follows a freemium model. A free tier must exist because users will not pay for an app they have never tried. The free tier gives enough value to demonstrate the app’s quality, but limits usage enough to drive upgrades. There are no human tutors involved in the initial version. Human tutoring may be considered as a future premium tier once the product is established, but it is out of scope for launch due to the cost and complexity of hiring qualified instructors. Revenue purpose Monetisation is not just a business goal — it is necessary for scaling. API costs (AI, speech-to-text, text-to-speech) grow with usage, and the founder intends to expand the product beyond Sri Lanka to other underserved language communities. Revenue is the engine that makes this possible. Tech Stack Mobile frontend The app is built with React Native and Expo — one codebase covering both iOS and Android. React Native was chosen over native Swift/Kotlin because the founder is a solo developer and maintaining two separate native codebases simultaneously is not practical. Voice and audio needs are handled via expo-av and react-native-voice, with native modules available as a fallback if the audio pipeline requires it. Framework: React Native with Expo (TypeScript throughout) Navigation: Expo Router (filesystem based) State management: Zustand Audio capture and playback: expo-av Voice input: react-native-voice Backend Firebase is the chosen backend platform. It removes the need to manage servers and scales automatically with user growth. No N x or Turbo repo — just disciplined folder naming. Data storage Firestore is the primary database. Key collections are users (profile, subscription, streak), daily Usage (per-user per-day for free tier enforcement), sessions (one per conversation), messages sub-collection (in-session context only), and language Pairs (config enabling future language expansion). AI memory model Full conversation history is passed to the LLM within a session (last 20 messages). Longterm memory across sessions is stored as a structured ai Summary object — assessed level, frequent errors, topics covered, strengths, and recommended focus — not raw transcripts. The last 3 session summaries are passed to the AI at the start of each new session. Security model The server is the authority on everything. The client only captures audio, plays audio, and renders UI. Free tier limits, subscription status, and AI responses are all enforced server side via Cloud Functions using the Firebase Admin SDK, which bypasses Firestore security rules intentionally. Direct client writes to daily Usage are blocked. Speaking games (v1) Two game types at launch. First, a pronunciation challenge — the AI says a word or phrase and the user repeats it, scored on accuracy. Second, a sentence completion game — the AI gives a Sinhala prompt and the user responds in English. Both are premiumonly. Detailed game spec to be written before Phase H of development.
